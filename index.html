PhishNet AI

Everyday, millions of people fall victim to phishing attempts, scams, and identity theft. PhishNet AI catches these malicious websites and explains why they are dangerous using AI and anti-virus programs. This program comes in the form of a google chrome extension, easily accessible and usable for those even in older ages. With this program, we hoped to help not just everyone, but especially those in older ages who fall victim to these scams, and donâ€™t know how to prevent them otherwise. We hope that with our program, we can mitigate these scams. Me and my team of three other group members worked together over a span of the 2025 Bitcamp, or 2 days, to create this program, and we hope to expand upon it in the future as well.

In this program, I personally worked on the backend, making sure that our program could properly communicate with the other programs already in place to make our app feasible. For example, part of what we used were anti-virus APIs which allowed us to access an already large database and check with those databases to see whether websites that were being accessed had scams in them. Additionally, as part of those anti-virus APIs, they also used their own plethora of programs that checked for general scams/phishing links. Looking into these APIs, I was able to connect them to the main frontend application, which is what you see in the google extension. .
